# Analytics & Conversations

Once your bot is live and interacting with visitors, you can track its performance and read the actual conversations taking place.

![Analytics Dashboard showing message volume charts and statistics](/docs-images/analytics.png)

## Viewing Analytics

Navigate to **Analytics** in the sidebar. This dashboard gives you a high-level view of how much your bot is being used.

You can view key metrics such as:
*   **Total Conversations:** The number of unique chat threads initiated by users.
*   **Total Messages:** The total volume of back-and-forth messages sent.
*   **Average Conversation Length:** How many messages are typically in a single thread.

You can use this data to understand peak usage times and overall engagement with your AI assistant.

---

## Reading User Conversations

While analytics give you the big picture, reading actual conversations helps you understand *what* your users are asking.

1.  Navigate to **Conversations** in the sidebar.
2.  You will see a chronologically ordered list of all chat sessions.
3.  Click on any conversation in the list to view the full transcript on the right side of the screen.

![Conversations view showing a list of chats on the left and a full transcript on the right](/docs-images/conversations.png)

### Why should you read conversations?

This is one of the most powerful features of GyaanChat. By reviewing chat logs, you can:

1.  **Identify gaps in your knowledge base:** If users keep asking a question that the bot doesn't know the answer to, that's a direct signal that you need to upload a new document covering that topic.
2.  **Improve your prompts:** If the bot is answering correctly but the tone is wrong, you can tweak the AI Personality prompt.
3.  **Understand your customers better:** You get unsolicited, real-time feedback on what visitors to your website actually care about or need help with.

> [!NOTE]
> All conversations are stored securely. You can delete individual chat logs at any time if you wish to remove them from your history.
