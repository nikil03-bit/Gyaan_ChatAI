# Uploading Documents

Your documents are the foundation of your AI bot. Everything the bot knows and can talk about comes from the files you upload here. The more clear and well-organized your documents are, the better your bot will perform.

## How to Upload a File

1.  Go to **Knowledge Base** in the left sidebar.
2.  Click the upload area or **drag and drop** your file directly onto the page.

![Knowledge Base page showing the Drag and Drop upload area](/docs-images/documents.png)

3.  Wait a few seconds while GyaanChat processes and indexes your file.
4.  Once indexed, your file will appear in the document list with a green status indicator.

![Document List showing uploaded files with their active status](/docs-images/documents.png)

That's it! Your bot will immediately start using that file to answer questions.

> [!NOTE]
> Processing usually takes just a few seconds. For very large documents (close to the file size limit), it may take up to a minute.

## What File Types Are Supported?

Currently, GyaanChat supports:
*   `.pdf` (Portable Document Format)
*   `.docx` (Microsoft Word)
*   `.txt` (Plain text files)

We are constantly working to add support for more file types in the future.

## Tips for Better Results

The golden rule of AI chatbots is: **quality in, quality out**.

### Use text-based PDFs
If you're uploading a PDF, make sure the text can be highlighted and copied. Scanned images of documents (where the text is just a picture) cannot be read by the AI unless they have been processed with OCR (Optical Character Recognition) first.

### Keep your documents organized
Remove irrelevant title pages, table of contents, or long legal disclaimers if they aren't necessary for answering user questions.

### Upload focused content
One focused document (e.g., a product FAQ) will produce better, more accurate results than a single massive file covering dozens of unrelated topics.

## Update documents when things change

If your information changes, delete the old file and upload a new version. The bot will immediately start using the updated content.

## Managing Your Documents

![Managing documents showing the delete button next to a file](/docs-images/documents.png)

*   To **delete** a document, click the trash icon next to it in the list.
*   Deleting a document removes it from the bot's knowledge immediately.
*   You can upload **multiple documents** — the bot searches across all of them.

> [!WARNING]
> Once a document is deleted, it cannot be recovered. Make sure you have a local copy before deleting.
